Question Think Respond Reflect

More than a randomizer

Use selection as part of a planned learning conversation.

Cambridge guidance places student thinking, questioning, feedback, reflection, and responsive teaching at the centre of effective lessons. Random Student Selector supports that work by turning a common classroom moment into a repeatable routine: prepare the class, hear from a wider range of learners, capture what happened, and adapt the next move.

Cambridge fit

Aligned with active learning, assessment for learning, and metacognition.

The app works best when the question is planned against a learning objective and success criteria. It gives the teacher a practical way to check understanding repeatedly, include more learners, and use informal evidence to decide whether to scaffold, extend, reteach, or move on.

Teaching loop

A simple rhythm for questioning that leads somewhere.

01

Ask from the objective

Start with a question linked to the learning goal, not with the random selection itself.

02

Protect thinking time

Use the timer so learners can retrieve, rehearse, compare ideas, or prepare evidence.

03

Select and probe

Choose from the present roster, then use follow-up questions to reveal reasoning and misconceptions.

04

Record and respond

Capture a quick outcome and use the pattern to adjust the next explanation, task, or grouping.

Teacher-facing interface

A compact dock for live classroom decisions.

The interface keeps the lesson state visible: active class, attendance, timer, selection status, sound controls, and session summary. It is built for teachers who need to keep attention on the room, not on the software.

Feature set

Tools for equitable participation and usable formative evidence.

Attendance-aware roster

Roll call removes absent students from the active selection pool so classroom questioning stays credible.

Visible wait time

Timer presets create a preparation window before the selected learner is expected to respond.

Quick formative outcomes

Record A*, A, B, C, No Grade, or Absent as lightweight evidence from classroom dialogue.

Session memory

Chosen and absent students stay excluded while the app remains open, helping participation spread.

Reflection summary

Review remaining learners, absences, grade-band counts, and follow-up signals after or during the lesson.

Classroom-ready layout

High-DPI, always-on-top, and multi-monitor behavior support real teaching spaces and quick transitions.
